BIO

Dr Yuquan (Bill) Du is a Senior Lecturer of Logistics and Supply Chain Management in the Department of Management and Marketing, La Trobe Business School (LBS). His current research concentrates on applying machine learning, simulation, and optimisation (operations research) approaches to the decision-making problems in transport, logistics, supply chain, and value chain systems. He is a highly cited researcher in the area of global freight transport and logistics studies. Some of his studies have gained high academic or industrial reputation, such as INFORMS President’s Pick (2015), and Industry Mention of IBM’s Optimisation Team on CPLEX. One of his papers also ranks in the MOST CITED ARTICLES since 2011 of Transportation Research Part E. He has been the Lead Applicant/Chief Investigator/Academic Collaborator of research projects with a total funding in excess of $1 million. His research efforts contribute mainly to United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) on Goal 9 (Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure), Goal 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production), and Goal 13 (Climate Action). He is a world-leading researcher in carbon accounting and reporting for transport, logistics, and supply chain activities. Notably, he has been conducting cutting-edge research on energy and CO2 emission efficiency of transport vehicles such as ships and trucks by utilising state-of-the-art AI and machine learning models. In this regard, his research has been supported by industry partners including American President Lines Ltd (APL), Milaha Maritime and Logistics, and Kongsberg Digital (KDI), especially in the form of providing industry datasets and insights. This research strength also expands to freight truck electrification and decarbonisation of warehousing and distribution activities. His research interests also extend from supply chain management to a wide range of value chain management topics, such as AgriBusiness. For instance, funded by AgriFutures Australia, he developed Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG) models to quantify and predict the risks and disruptions facing small and medium agribusinesses, such as farms producing gingers, cherries, and fodder. He also co-supervised a PhD student who developed mathematical programming models and algorithms to tackle the decision makings in vegetable production planning and relevant logistics arrangements, such as crop rotation, cropping plan, and harvest schedule. He also regarded himself a coder (computer programmer) who speaks C++, Julia, Python, Matlab, and VBA (for spreadsheet) to computers. Therefore, he is also particularly interested in emerging and disruptive technologies, such as blockchain, digital twins, AI, and IoT technologies. For instance, he co-supervised a PhD study that investigates the risks associated with the adoption of blockchain in container transport systems. Dr Du obtained his B.E. in Software Engineering in 2004 at Nankai University in China. Then, he worked as a lecturer and a technical specialist in Jiangxi Vocational College of Finance and Economics between 2004 and 2006. After the two-year work, he returned to Nankai University to pursue his master's and doctor's degrees. He obtained his PhD in Control Theory and Control Engineering (Logistics and Supply Chain Management) in June 2012. Afterwards, he worked for about one year as an Assistant Professor at the Department of Transportation Engineering in Inner Mongolia University (IMU). Motivated by his academic aspiration, in Nov 2013, he joined the Centre for Maritime Studies (CMS), National University of Singapore (NUS), and worked there as a Research Fellow for three years. For 6+ years from Oct 2016, he was working as a lecturer/senior lecturer with Australian Maritime College (AMC), University of Tasmania (UTAS). He joined La Trobe Business School, La Trobe University on Feb 2023 starting a new chapter in Melbourne. PhD applications are welcome that apply machine learning, simulation, and optimisation approaches to decision marking problems in transport, logistics, supply chain, and value chain (especially agribusiness) systems.
